## How This Meets the Rubric (40 Points)

### 1. **Purpose: Persuasive Purpose and Key Points are Clear** ✅

**Key Message:** "Your 4th year of math actually changes your future"

The artifact reframes the essay's policy argument into a student-centered message. Instead of arguing to Congress, it persuades students by connecting the essay's evidence to their real lives:

- **Key Point 1:** Math builds critical thinking skills that apply to ANY career
- **Key Point 2:** Data literacy protects you from misinformation and manipulation
- **Key Point 3:** You have flexibility in WHICH math you take (not a one-size-fits-all requirement)
- **Key Point 4:** Everyone needs these skills, regardless of college plans

The headline immediately states the purpose: "Why Your 4th Year of Math Actually Changes Your Future"

### 2. **Audience: Artifact is Appropriate for Intended Audience & Real-World Usage** ✅

**Original Audience:** Congress (policy brief format)  
**New Audience:** High school students (ages 14-18)

**Format chosen = Website/Interactive Infographic**

Why this works:
- Students consume information via web pages and social media, not policy briefs
- Interactive elements (tabs, stat cards, myth/fact boxes) keep engagement high
- Mobile-responsive design works on phones/tablets students actually use
- Shareable format students could screenshot and share on social media
- Can be printed as a poster or incorporated into school materials

**Real-world use cases:**
- School counselors can link to this when discussing 4-year math requirements
- Students can access this before scheduling courses
- Teachers can use it to motivate students about math's real-world value
- Parents can show students why math matters for their future

### 3. **Strategies: Artifact Addresses Ethos, Logos, and Pathos** ✅

#### **LOGOS (Logic & Evidence)**
- **2.4 point ACT increase** - displays prominently in stat cards with context
- **64% of students report math helped in other subjects** - hard data visualization
- **60% use math weekly** - drives home that it's not just theory
- **Survey of 25 respondents** - cited as primary research backing the claims
- **4 professional sources cited** with explanations of what each shows
- **Specific course recommendations** (Statistics, Financial Math, AP Calc) ground the argument in reality

#### **ETHOS (Credibility & Authority)**
- **Quotes from real students** - "The most important reason students should take four years of math is..."
- **Quote from academic authority** - Mathematics Professor Robert Lewis lending credibility
- **All sources cited with attribution** - ACT.org, EdSource/LAUSD study, Lewis, primary research
- **Professional design & layout** - consistent branding with your existing site (Nile blue, clean typography)
- **Academic perspective** - treats the topic seriously while being accessible

#### **PATHOS (Emotion & Personal Connection)**
- **"You think you'll never use calculus again"** - acknowledges student skepticism
- **Real-life scenarios** - Finance/money, healthcare, trades, creative fields show math's role in actual careers
- **Personal stakes framed** - "This isn't about forcing math on you. It's about making sure you graduate with skills to..."
- **Fear of manipulation** - "One misleading graph away from a bad decision" / "someone who gets played by it"
- **Empathy for concerns** - validating student worries about stress, schedule conflicts, relevance
- **Call-to-action to explore your path** - emotionally connects to student agency and choice
